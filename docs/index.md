# The Balanced Scorecard Process (DEV PREVIEW)

*A book on a methodology for surfacing, measuring, and balancing the competing values an organization is actually operating under.*

**By John G. Tittle** — JGT Transformation Engineering

---

## What this book is

Most organizations operate under tensions they have not made explicit. Productivity competes with quality. Short-term commitments compete with long-term value. The customer relationship competes with the contract. When these tensions are unnamed, organizations chase whichever value is loudest at any given moment, hit the targets they wrote down, and still leave the customer dissatisfied — because the targets were not measuring the things the customer was actually paying for.

The Balanced Scorecard Process (BSCP) is a structured way to surface these tensions, agree on the relative weight each one carries, define metrics that reflect those weights, and build a one-page report that any line manager can use to know what to focus on this week and this month. The methodology was originally developed in outsourcing-consulting work, where the gap between contractually-green service-level agreements and visibly-unhappy customers was the daily problem to solve. It has since been applied across industries.

This book documents the process — where it came from, how it works step by step, what it looks like when applied to real client situations, how to know if it's the right tool for your context, and what's ahead.
