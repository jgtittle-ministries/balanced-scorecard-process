# Appendix: Metric Options Reference

!!! info "Reference catalog from production BSCP engagements"
    This appendix is a catalog of metric specifications drawn from production BSCP engagements at three sanitized organizations (Company ABC America, Company DEF, and Company ABC Canada). Each metric entry follows the full BSCP specification template — metric name, definition, business purpose, expected behavior, target, algorithm, calculation period, base measures with inclusions/exclusions, data generation/collection/storage, reporting frequency, aggregation rules, and scoring rules with weights.

    Use this appendix as a reference when defining metrics for a BSCP engagement of your own. Each entry shows what a fully-developed metric specification looks like in practice, including the level of operational detail required for a metric to be implementable.

    *Sanitized version 1.1 (October 2015). Account-specific data has been replaced with placeholder names (`Company ABC America`, `Company DEF`, `Company ABC Canada`, and `SERVICES PROVIDER` for the vendor). Two original figures (in the Critical-Roadmap-Projects scoring sections) were exported from Word as vector graphics that do not display in web browsers; they are flagged inline below.*

## How this appendix is organized

The catalog is grouped by source organization. Each metric belongs to one of the standard BSCP categories (Service Quality, Productivity Enablers, Project Delivery, Client Satisfaction, etc.) — the category name appears as a sub-heading above each metric group.

## Resolution Time of Severity 1 Application Problems

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Metric Name** | Resolution Time of Severity 1 Application Problems |
| **Definition** | The % application problem tickets resolved within the service target, measured from the time the ticket is entered by the Help Desk until the ticket is closed or a workaround is provided to restore service to the End-Users. |
| **Business Purpose/Goal** | Critical operational problems are quickly resolved |
| **Expected Behavior** | Appropriate number of SERVICES PROVIDER resources are dedicated to resolve the problem. |
| **Metric Target** | 90% of application Severity 1 problems resolved in 4 hours |
| **Industry Reference and/or Standard** | Not Applicable |
| **Metric Algorithm** | For the calculation period:  **Resolution Time of Severity 1 Application Problems** =  Number of Severity 1 tickets resolved within service target  \* 100  Total number of Severity 1 tickets    ***Numeric score will be calculated as a percentage to 2 decimal places*** |
| **Calculation Period** | The metric is calculated monthly (based on the financial month) |
| **Base Measures** | ***Severity* *1***: Per the COMPANY ABC Information Technology Services Agreement Schedule 4.13 Service Level Agreements (Attachment N)  ***Resolution*:** Per the COMPANY ABC Information Technology Services Agreement Schedule 4.13 Service Level Agreements (Attachment N)  ***Resolution Time*** = the elapsed time from the time that the ticket was opened until the ticket was marked resolved (“fixed” status), less time spent in “pending” status for conditions caused by the customer. Pending time includes times when a workaround is in place. Any pending times will be documented in the ticket comments.  ***Severity 1 tickets resolved within 4 hours*** the number of closed Severity 1 tickets resolved within 4 hours  ***Excused downtime***: as defined in the Agreement  ***Scheduled Downtime****:* downtime approved by the Change Control Board and published in the Production Schedule. Scheduled Downtime includes standard (routine) maintenance, e.g. backups  **Inclusions:**   - Closed Severity 1 application problem tickets - Database problem tickets   **Exclusions:**   - Any non-closed problem tickets at the time of reporting. - Time outside the established Service Window - Duplicate problem tickets for the same problem occurrence - Scheduled Downtime - Non-production system problems - ERP Severity 1 tickets when "go-live" production date is prior to SERVICES PROVIDER production acceptance. - Any Severity 1 tickets generated as a result of work prior to SERVICES PROVIDER production acceptance.     **Special Notes:**   - ERP tickets counted after ERP release meets acceptance criteria - If all members of a redundant configuration are down, this counts as a single outage, not as an outage for each member in the redundant configuration. - All times measured in Eastern time. - SERVICES PROVIDER shall be entitled to Service compliance relief per Section 4.13.3 of the Agreement. |
| **Data Generation** | Application problem ticket data is generated in the Remedy Problem Management system and is updated according to the Problem Management Process. Data validity is the responsibility of the GIS Production Operations at initial ticket entry, and the Problem Ticket Owner for ongoing updates to the ticket as it progresses through the problem management process. |
| **Data Collection** | Application problem ticket data is extracted from the Remedy Problem Management system and loaded into Microsoft Excel Spreadsheets / CSV files. |
| **Data Storage** | Application problem ticket data is stored on a COMPANY ABC shared file server (for data review / audit ) and in the SERVICES PROVIDER Metrics Work Product Repository |
| **Report** | The Metrics Analyst extracts the data, calculates the metric, and reports the data via the COMPANY ABC Balanced Scorecard. |
| **Reporting Frequency** | - Interim report issued in month 1 and 2 of the quarter - Scored report issued in month 3 of the quarter |
| **Aggregation Rules and Examples** | **Single calculation period (1 month):**  Ticket 1 – Resolved within 4 hours  Ticket 2 – Resolved within 4 hours  Ticket 3 – Not resolved within 4 hours  1+1+0  x 100 = 67.7%  3  **Multiple calculation periods:** combine additional ticket numbers with those from the previous calculation period(s) included in-scope for the scoring period. Use raw numbers and do not “average” averages  Ticket 4 – Resolved within 4 hours  Ticket 5 – Resolved within 4 hours  Ticket 6 – Resolved within 4 hours  1+1+0+1+1+1  x 100 = 83.3%  6 |
| **Scoring Rules, Weights, and Scoring Frequency** | **Rules**:   - The metric score is converted to a numeric score between 0 and 1 based on a series of linear equations that form a piece-wise linear graph. The data points used in the equation are:   o Low 60%= 0.0 score  o Pass 90% = .80 score  o High 100% = 1.0 score   - Minimum Data Points: N/A - If zero Severity 1 tickets occur in the scoring period, the metric will be scored as N/A, and its metric weight will be redistributed across the other scoring metrics.   **Weights:**   - Category Weight: 22% - Metric Weight within category: 20% - Overall Balanced Scorecard Weight: 4.40%   **Scored:**   - Scored each quarter on cumulative data for the quarter-to-date - No Baseline period required before initial scoring. |

## Skill Base (Technical)

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Measure Name** | Skill Base (Technical) |
| **Definition** | Index measuring the technical skill base of the Business Unit based (assigned to business unit and charge more than half of billable) compared to baseline technical skill base. |
| **Business Purpose/Goal** | Maintain a stable mix of currently needed technical skills |
| **Expected Behavior** | Encourage SERVICES PROVIDER to invest in skill mix against forecast technology needs. |
| **Metric Target** | Skill Index > 2.131  Revisit target after Dec 2002 |
| **Industry Reference and/or Standard** | Not Applicable |
| **Metric Algorithm** | For the calculation period:  **Skill Base (Technical)** =  Sum of the technical skill values  Number of technical skills  ***Numeric score will be calculated as number to 3 decimal place*** |
| **Calculation Period** | The metric is calculated every 6 months (June, December) |
| **Base Measures** | ***Five categories of skill level:***   - ***Not Applicable***-skill not applicable to role - ***Conceptual/Trained* -** understands the concepts and foundational concepts - ***Experienced*** - familiarity gained through experience or association and has the capability to apply techniques - ***Expert -*** ability to apply the functional and technical knowledge and skills to do the job at a high level of accomplishment and can train others in skill - ***Guru -*** consistently deployed, implemented and applied a specific skill to support the business requirements for a minimum of 10 years or the maximum of the existence of the skill.   ***Skill Level Index Values:***   - *Conceptual /Trained* = **1** - *Experienced* = **2** (converted from 3 in Skill database) - *Expert* = **3** (converted from 4 in Skill database) - *Guru* = **4** (converted from 5 in Skill database)   ***Technical Skills***: Skills that support COMPANY ABC’s current business needs or strategic initiatives and must be maintained / developed during the current reporting year.  **Inclusions:**   - Technical skill values for business unit personnel   **Exclusions:**   - Non-technical skills - Skills scored Not-Applicable   **Special Notes:**   - Skill base index may be re-baselined with the addition / deletion of Technical Skills Competency Areas**.** - SERVICES PROVIDER shall be entitled to Service compliance relief per Section 4.13.3 of the Agreement. - Records for technical skill base associated with individuals shall not generally be available to COMPANY ABC, however may be subject to audits per Section 17 (Recordkeeping and Audit Rights) of the Agreement. - In the beginning of the reporting year, the SERVICES PROVIDER Account Delivery Manager (ADM) or designee will submit the current skill list (extracted from the George Training and Skills Assessment module) to the client for review. The client will identify the technical skill competency areas that COMPANY ABC wants maintained / developed and return it to the ADM within 30 days. Inclusions and exclusions on the list are negotiated and agreed upon between COMPANY ABC and SERVICES PROVIDER. The finalized list will be used to calculate the individual technical index values. The list is stored with other baseline documents for the current reporting year in the Metrics Work Product Repository. |
