# Training Appendix: Lessons Learned from Delivering the BSCP Training

!!! info "Lessons-learned notes from the BSCP training delivery"
    This appendix collects observations from delivering the BSCP training series to consulting teams — what worked, what did not, what to do differently next time. Many of the items here are short notes; the ones with substantive prose are kept; the bare bullet-only notes are flagged in an Author's notes section at the bottom for transparency.

## What went well

- **Hold to one hour.** Keep training sessions short and focused. The international scheduling window made longer sessions hard to find anyway.
- **Interaction with questions.** Live questions during sessions consistently produced the deepest engagement and the clearest signal about which parts of the methodology people were actually grasping.
- **Presentations available early on a shared portal.** Posting the slide material before sessions let participants review and come with their questions, raising the level of the discussion.
- **Cultural difference helpful with the scoring model.** Cross-cultural teams produced more thoughtful pairwise comparisons in the AHP weighting work — the differences in framing forced clearer articulation of what was being traded off.
- **Insights and depth of discussions.** The training sessions consistently went deeper than slide content alone — the conversations around the slides were the actual training.

## What did not go so well — and what to do differently

- **Participation was uneven.** A few participants drove most of the discussion; others were quiet. Future deliveries should plan for active facilitation to draw out the quieter participants and consider a smaller-group format.
- **Record sessions and post with the briefing.** A recorded version of each session, posted alongside the slides on the shared portal, would let absent or late participants catch up without taking time from the next live session.
- **The Accelerating Change (IMA) material was too shallow as delivered.** It worked for participants who were already looking for the organizational-change framing of the methodology, but not enough depth to let new participants actually apply the tools.
- **The Expert Choice tool needed to be shown working.** The first session went too far into the math of pairwise comparisons before showing the tool itself. The second session reversed the order — demo first, theory second — and worked much better. Don't lead with the theory; lead with the tool, then come back to the theory once the operation is concrete.
- **Standardize presentation style.** Fonts, templates, common look-and-feel across decks; guidelines around use of animation. Inconsistency across decks distracted from the methodology content.
- **Define target audiences explicitly.** There appear to be three audience levels: (1) overview seekers who want a marketing-level briefing; (2) practitioner-adjacent who want first-level detail on each part of the methodology without necessarily expecting to deliver it; (3) team members who will actually be running the process. Each audience needs a different agenda.
- **Schedule a week off periodically.** Two weeks on, one week off — and use the off week to catch up with participants individually. The continuous-pace schedule produced fatigue and dropped attendance. The alternating schedule also accommodates participants in non-overlapping time zones.

## What we need to do next

- More Accelerating Change training so the organizational-change side of the methodology is delivered with the depth it deserves.
- Expert Choice tool demo as a standard part of every delivery.
- A notebook with a brief process description and some marketing-level material for prospects and stakeholders who do not need the full training.
- A training catalog to record who has been trained and at what level.
- More practical-experience opportunities — ride-alongs on live BSCP engagements as part of training.
