## Slide 5: 5

![Slide 5](implementation-plan/slide-005.png)

**Estimating: Impact**

- Low
- data is currently available
- or metric is already being calculated
- or metric can be automatically generated from existing data source
- one or two people can generate metrics without other org change
- metrics is already benchmarked by the firm
- or generally accepted standards exist of acceptable performance
- current performance and trending are well understood
- accurate measurement is well-incented
- little or no effort to implement


## Slide 6: 6

![Slide 6](implementation-plan/slide-006.png)

**Estimating**

- Medium
- data can be gathered as a part of existing processes
- limited group of people need to be involved
- data has available benchmarks
- similar measures are taken in the industry
- data exists or can be somewhat easily be gathered to establish current performance
- no disincentive exists to collect accurate measures
- disinterested third party (e.g. QA or Raytheon) collects data
- over 80 hours to implement


## Slide 7: 7

![Slide 7](implementation-plan/slide-007.png)

**Estimating**

- High
- new processes are needed
- many the firm/Raytheon people will have to change behavior
- no similar measure is done at the firm or Raytheon
- little or no benchmark information is understood
- no current data exists or can be modeled to establish current performance
- this is an unusual measure within the industry
- metrics data must be hand gathered or manually correlated
- measure requires performer to self-report poor performance
- measures are subjective or subject to interpretation
- High emotion or dollar value is associated with outcome of direct measure

## Slide 15: 15

![Slide 15](implementation-plan/slide-015.png)

**Release Plan**

- How quick can we get stuff out
- Keeps from being a big bang effect
- Starts behavior changes even before collecting metrics
- builds on what they know from previous releases
- Split measures into releases based on simplicity of implementation (easier right away, more difficult down the line)
- Traditionally T0, T1, T3 (immediately available, available soon, difficult to measure)
- First release should have >= 50% weighting and at most, 1 category without coverage
- Things you can do quickly
- Second release should have minor process impact
- Third release will have major process and tool impact
